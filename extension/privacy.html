Daily Mirror — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

Daily Mirror is built to be a private, on-device tool. We do not operate any server, and we never receive, store, or have access to your data.

What is stored, and where

  • Your browsing activity (page URLs, titles, and measured active time) is stored only in your browser, in local IndexedDB. It never leaves your device except as described below.
  • Your settings (OpenRouter API key, chosen model, and your weekly goals) are stored only in your browser, in chrome.storage.local.

The one time data leaves your device

When you request a daily summary (by clicking “Summarize”, or via the optional evening auto-summary), the day’s activity is sent directly from your browser to OpenRouter (openrouter.ai), using your own API key, so that an AI model can write your narrative. This is the only outbound network request the extension makes. Your data is handled according to OpenRouter’s and the underlying model provider’s policies. We are not a party to that transfer and never see it.

What we do NOT do

  • No analytics, telemetry, or tracking of any kind.
  • No servers, accounts, or cloud sync operated by us.
  • No selling, sharing, or transmitting your data to us or any third party (other than the AI provider you configured, at your request).
  • No screenshots, keystroke logging, or capture of page contents beyond URL and title.

Permissions

  • history / tabs / idle — to measure how you spend time across sites, locally. Idle detection ensures we don’t count time when you’re away.
  • storage — to keep your activity and settings on your device.
  • openrouter.ai host access — to send the summary request you initiate.

Deleting your data

You can clear all stored activity at any time by removing the extension, or by clearing the extension’s site data in your browser. Uninstalling deletes everything.
